## Overview

**Sanad Care** is a professional static website I designed and developed as an **expert frontend developer** for a home nursing and healthcare service based in Marrakech, Morocco. The site serves as the digital front door for Sanad Care, connecting local residents and international medical tourists with qualified, state-registered nurses providing 24/7 home healthcare services.

## The Problem

Before this website, Sanad Care faced significant visibility and trust challenges:

- **No digital presence** — Potential patients and families couldn't find or verify the service online
- **Medical tourism gap** — International visitors in Marrakech had no way to discover English-speaking home nursing
- **Trust deficit** — Without a professional website, credibility was questioned compared to competitors
- **Missed emergency inquiries** — 24/7 service was invisible to people searching at night or during crises
- **Competitor advantage** — Other home care services had basic websites capturing search traffic

## My Role

As the **expert frontend developer**, I was responsible for:

- Complete website design and development from scratch
- Local SEO strategy targeting Marrakech healthcare searches
- Medical tourism content optimization for international visitors
- Performance optimization for users on mobile and variable connections
- Multi-page architecture covering all services and patient needs
- Trust-building design that conveys professionalism and compassion

## SEO Strategy

### Local SEO for Marrakech Healthcare

- **Primary keywords**: "home nursing Marrakech," "private nurse Marrakech," "home care Morocco"
- **Service keywords**: "blood test at home Marrakech," "IV therapy home Marrakech," "elderly care Marrakech"
- **Medical tourism keywords**: "nurse for tourists Marrakech," "medical assistance hotel Marrakech," "English speaking nurse Morocco"
- **Long-tail keywords**: "24 hour nursing care Marrakech," "post surgery care at home Morocco," "wound dressing change home visit"

### Technical SEO

- **Schema.org markup** — MedicalBusiness, LocalBusiness, Service, and FAQPage structured data
- **Sitemap.xml** — Automatic generation with priority and changefreq
- **Robots.txt** — Optimized crawl directives
- **Canonical URLs** — Prevent duplicate content across language versions
- **Core Web Vitals** — All metrics in "Good" range for ranking signals

### Content SEO

- **Service-specific landing pages** — Each service has dedicated, keyword-rich content
- **Patient testimonials** — Social proof with Schema.org Review markup
- **FAQ schema** — Rich snippets for common questions
- **Local citations** — Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across web
- **Google Business Profile** — Linked and optimized for local pack

## Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge 1: Building Trust in Healthcare

Healthcare decisions require absolute confidence. I implemented:

- **Nurse credential display** — Registration numbers, certifications, experience
- **Patient photo testimonials** — Real faces and stories (with consent)
- **Transparent process** — Step-by-step explanation of how care works
- **Safety protocols** — Detailed hygiene and security measures
- **Insurance information** — Clarity on coverage and payment options

### Challenge 2: Medical Tourism Content

International visitors have unique concerns. I created:

- **English-first content** — Primary language for tourist audience
- **Hotel/riad care specifics** — Understanding of tourist accommodation
- **Rapid response promise** — Fast dispatch for time-sensitive visitors
- **Doctor coordination** — Partnership with local physicians for referrals
- **Cultural sensitivity** — Respect for diverse backgrounds and needs

### Challenge 3: Performance on Variable Connections

Patients and families may browse on slow connections. I optimized:

- **Static generation** — Instant page loads, no server wait time
- **Image optimization** — WebP format, responsive sizes, lazy loading
- **Minimal JavaScript** — Core functionality works without JS
- **Progressive enhancement** — Rich features load progressively
- **Offline-ready** — Service Worker for basic offline access

### Challenge 4: Accessibility for Elderly Users

Many patients and their families are older adults. I ensured:

- **Large touch targets** — 48px minimum for all interactive elements
- **High contrast** — WCAG AA compliant color combinations
- **Readable typography** — Minimum 16px body text, clear hierarchy
- **Screen reader support** — Proper ARIA labels and semantic HTML
- **Keyboard navigation** — Full site usable without mouse
